Company Overview
**The Pulse Broadcasting Network (PulseNet)** is the premier entertainment division of **The Exelus Corporation**, dedicated to delivering music, visual media, and cultural storytelling across the United Empire of Earth and beyond.

Through an extensive web of comm relays, spectrum channels, and localized broadcast nodes, PulseNet distributes everything from independent musicians and frontier storytellers to polished corporate productions and live cultural events.

Whether a citizen is tuning in from a luxury apartment on Terra, a refueling station in Pyro, or a cargo hauler drifting between jump points, PulseNet aims to ensure one thing remains constant:

*The ‘Verse always has a soundtrack.*

PulseNet’s programming blends:

- Music networks covering every major genre in the UEE
- Live performance broadcasts
- Documentary videography from across - explored space
- Cultural archives preserving pre-Messers Earth media
- Photography and visual storytelling from explorers, journalists, and artists

Over five decades, PulseNet has become one of the most recognizable entertainment networks in the Empire.

## Corporate History
### 2905 – Founding

PulseNet was founded in **2905** as a small experimental broadcast initiative within **The Exelus Corporation**, originally intended to distribute promotional media and cultural programming to corporate clients.

At the time, most entertainment distribution still relied heavily on **localized planetary networks** and **commercial entertainment providers**. Exelus recognized an opportunity to create something larger: a **multi-system entertainment broadcast network** capable of delivering music and visual media anywhere comm signals could reach.

The early PulseNet network consisted of only three broadcast relays and a single music channel.

Within five years, its audience had grown far beyond corporate clientele.

### 2910 – The First Expansion

As civilian traffic between systems increased, PulseNet began establishing **relay partnerships with major comm arrays**, allowing broadcasts to travel alongside navigation and commercial signals.

This expansion enabled PulseNet to launch its first genre-focused music divisions:

- **Trailstar** – frontier country and spacer folk
- **NovaBeat** – electronic and synthwave music popular in core systems
- **IronChord **– rock and industrial metal

For the first time, citizens on distant worlds could listen to the **same music broadcasts simultaneously across multiple systems**.

### 2918 – The Visual Era

Recognizing the growing demand for visual media, PulseNet expanded beyond audio broadcasts into **videography and photography networks**.

The division launched **PulseVision**, a visual programming service featuring:

- Music videos
- Cultural documentaries
- System travelogues
- Live concert recordings
- Photojournalism from explorers and independent reporters

This era cemented PulseNet as a **full-spectrum entertainment network** rather than simply a music broadcaster.

### 2927 – The Citizen Creator Program

In **2927**, PulseNet introduced one of its most influential initiatives: the Citizen Creator Program.

Under this program, independent artists, pilots, explorers, and photographers could submit original media for broadcast consideration.

The results reshaped PulseNet’s identity.

Instead of relying solely on corporate productions, the network began showcasing:

- Cargo haulers recording life in deep space
- Frontier musicians performing in asteroid bars
- Photographers documenting newly charted systems
- Journalists covering the lives of settlers and miners

PulseNet became known as **the network that broadcasts the voice of the everyday citizen**.

### 2938 – Inter-System Broadcast Standardization

By **2938**, PulseNet had become large enough that inconsistent relay protocols began causing broadcast delays across distant systems.

In response, Exelus funded the development of **PulseSync**, a broadcast compression and synchronization protocol allowing media to travel efficiently through existing comm infrastructure.

PulseSync allowed PulseNet to:

- Deliver live concerts across systems
- Synchronize music releases Empire-wide
- Broadcast major cultural events in real time

Today, PulseSync remains the backbone of PulseNet’s broadcast technology.

### 2946 – Cultural Preservation Initiative

Recognizing the importance of preserving humanity’s artistic heritage, PulseNet launched the **Cultural Preservation Initiative in 2946**.

This project focused on restoring and digitizing historic media from:

- Old Earth archives
- Pre-Messer era recordings
- Early UEE broadcast history

Many classic works now rebroadcast on PulseNet channels originate from these restored archives.

### 2956 – Present Day

Now in its **51st year**, PulseNet stands as one of the most influential entertainment networks in the United Empire of Earth.

With hundreds of broadcast partners, thousands of independent contributors, and millions of daily listeners, the network continues to expand into new systems and frontier territories.

PulseNet programming now reaches:

- Core worlds
- Industrial systems
- Frontier colonies
- Long-haul shipping lanes
- Deep-space listening posts

From quiet acoustic performances drifting across comm channels to full-scale interstellar concert events, PulseNet remains committed to one mission:

**Bringing the music, stories, and images of humanity to every corner of the ‘Verse**.
