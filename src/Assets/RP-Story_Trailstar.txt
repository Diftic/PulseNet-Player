Trailstar
*Frontier Country • Spacer Folk*

Trailstar is PulseNet’s frontier music station, carrying the sound of long hauls, distant systems, and lives lived far from the core worlds. Rooted in country, folk, and acoustic traditions, the station blends Old Earth influences with modern spacer storytelling.

These are songs about:
- Cargo runs and jump routes
- Debts and second chances
- Empty stations and crowded bars
- Leaving… and coming back

Trailstar doesn’t rush.
It rides with you.

Station Tagline:
*“Songs for the Long Run.”*

## Station Identity
Trailstar represents the soul of the frontier.

It plays:
- Country (inspired by sounds from old Sol)
- Acoustic folk and spacer ballads
- Stripped-down live performances
- Storytelling-driven tracks

The station emphasizes **narrative and emotion**, often featuring songs that feel personal and lived-in.

## Audience

Trailstar is especially loved by:
- Cargo haulers and long-range pilots
- Frontier settlers and homesteaders
- Explorers traveling between systems
- Mechanics and station workers
- Anyone who prefers quiet over chaos

It’s the sound of people who spend more time **between destinations than at them**.

# DJ Profile
**Cassidy “Dustline” Rourke**

**Show**: *The Long Run*

Cassidy Rourke earned the nickname “Dustline” after years flying cargo routes through under-charted systems. After stepping away from piloting, she brought her experience to PulseNet, turning Trailstar into a station that feels authentic to those who live the life.

She doesn’t perform.
She remembers.

Dustline reads transmissions from listeners, shares stories from the black, and lets the music do most of the talking.

**Personality**:
- Calm and grounded
- Warm, steady voice
- Reflective and sincere
- Deeply connected to spacer life

**Signature Intro**:
*"This is Dustline on Trailstar.*
*If you’re out there running long… you’re not alone."*
